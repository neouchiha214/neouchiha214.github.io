Coffee Bean Choices

Coffee enthusiasts often find themselves captivated by the rich world of coffee beans, each type offering a unique flavor and characteristics. In this article, we will delve into the distinct features of four different coffee beans:

Arabica, Robusta, Excelsa and Liberica.

Arabica stands as the most widely used and cultivated coffee bean globally, contributing to almost 60-70% of coffee production. Known for its mild flavor and aromatic qualities, Arabica beans are often favored for their complex taste

Robusta beans are recognized for their higher caffeine content and a hint of chocolate flavor. However, some describe the taste as somewhat flat or possessing an almost burnt quality.

Excelsa coffee, previously categorized as a distinct variant, has recently been reclassified as a Liberica variant by scientists. These beans offer a lighter aroma and lower caffeine content compared to Arabica and Robusta.

Liberica coffee has earned a reputation for its controversial and polarizing nature, primarily due to its wild inconsistency

Traditional South Indian Filter coffee

This is prepared by blending heated milk and sugar with the infusionachieved through percolation brewing of finely powdered coffee along with chicory in a conventional Indian filter.

I regard this as the ultimate coffee experience simply because I’ve been enjoying this delightful beverage since my childhood. Yes I’m being biased.

There are multiple variations to this as well…it highly depends on the amount of chircory and the percentage of Decoction one uses during the preparation (although some of my friends from college might disagree on this) It can either enhance the coffee significantly or turn it into a disappointment.

I personally like the version with a slightly higher proportion of chicory to make it stronger.

Coffee Choices

black - the simplest of all…fresh coffee beans brewed with hot steaming water

espresso - is crafted using finely ground beans and a higher grounds-to-water ratio compared to regular coffee. This process yields a denser, more concentrated liquid with a more robust flavor.

latte - this traditional beverage usually consists of one-third espresso and two-thirds steamed milk, crowned with a delicate layer of foam.

cappuccino - this coffee creation, rooted in espresso, bears resemblance to a latte, yet distinguishes itself with a denser frothy top layer. The customary proportion comprises equal parts espresso, steamed milk, and foam.

americano - this is espresso but diluted.

mocha - are espresso-based drinks, featuring chocolate and whipped cream instead of foam, provide a delightful blend, offering a rich and satisfying experience with every sip.
